# Organizing with collections

A collection is a named group of images. Use collections to keep related shots together, like a haul-out, a delivery trip, or the electrical panel. An image can belong to several collections at once, so the same photo can live in **Repairs** and **Engine room** without being duplicated.

---

## Make a collection

Go to **Collections** and type a name, then select **Create**. The new collection appears in the list right away, empty and ready for images.

<p align="center"><img src="../images/collections.webp" alt="The Collections screen listing existing collections with a field to create a new one." width="85%"></p>

---

## Add images to a collection

Open an image in the **Library** to see its detail view. Each collection shows as a chip you can toggle. Tap a chip to add the image to that collection, tap it again to remove it. The change saves as soon as you toggle it.

<p align="center"><img src="../images/image-detail.webp" alt="An open image showing its details with collection chips you can toggle on or off." width="85%"></p>

> **Note:** An image can be in as many collections as you like. Toggling a chip off only removes the image from that collection, it does not delete the image.

---

## Filter the library by collection

Go to the **Library** and tap a collection chip in the toolbar. The grid then shows only the images in that collection. Tap the chip again to clear the filter and see everything.

---

## Rename or delete

Manage collections from the **Collections** screen. Rename a collection to update its label everywhere it is used. Delete a collection to remove the group itself.

> **Note:** Deleting a collection keeps every image in it. You are only removing the grouping, so the photos stay in your **Library**.
